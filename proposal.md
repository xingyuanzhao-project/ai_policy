# How AI Is Governed: Proactivity, Permissiveness, and Performance
Maps AI governance on ex ante vs ex post and promotion vs containment. Uses LLM classification of policy texts to cluster types and analyze drivers and performance using benchmark data, to guide security and competitiveness goals globally.

The project operationalizes national AI governance along two dimensions: timing of intervention and policy orientation toward containment or promotion. Drawing on the IAPP Global AI Law and Policy Tracker, the project implements an LLM-assisted classification pipeline applied to policy texts and status information, aggregates document-level classifications to jurisdiction-year with weights that reflect bindingness, legal status, and breadth, and then derives interpretable policy types through clustering. The first analysis examines what predicts policy choice by studying adoption dynamics and cross-sectional variation with diffusion and institutional covariates. The second analysis evaluates AI performance across the clustered policy types using public benchmark compilations, including the Hugging Face Open LLM Leaderboard and the OECD AIKoD database that links API endpoints to benchmark scores. This study provides decision-making evidence on how governance posture corresponds to observable AI capability, informing policy adjustments that balance security and competitiveness. The results help national and industry leaders benchmark their stance against higher-performing peers.
