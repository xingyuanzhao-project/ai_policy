CHAPTER ______

AN ACT concerning

Health Care - Prior Authorizations - Prohibiting Fees and Use of Artificial Intelligence

FOR the purpose of prohibiting certain insurers, nonprofit health service plans, and health maintenance organizations from using artificial intelligence to automatically deny prior authorizations; prohibiting certain health care providers from charging a fee to obtain a prior authorization from a carrier or managed care organization; and generally relating to prior authorizations.

BY adding to

Article - Health - General

Section 24-2501 and 24-2502 to be under the new subtitle "Subtitle 25. Fees for Prior Authorizations - Prohibited"

Annotated Code of Maryland

(2023 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

BY adding to

Article - Insurance

Section 15-1012

Annotated Code of Maryland

(2017 Replacement Volume and 2024 Supplement)

SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND, That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

Article - Health - General

SUBTITLE 25. FEES FOR PRIOR AUTHORIZATIONS-PROHIBITED.

24-2501.

(A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS INDICATED.

(B) "CARRIER" MEANS:

(1) A HEALTH INSURER;

(2) A NONPROFIT HEALTH SERVICE PLAN;

(3) A HEALTH MAINTENANCE ORGANIZATION; OR

(4) ANY OTHER ENTITY THAT PROVIDES HEALTH BENEFIT PLANS SUBJECT TO REGULATION BY THE STATE.

(C) "HEALTH CARE PROVIDER" MEANS:

(1) A HEALTH CARE PRACTITIONER REGULATED UNDER THE HEALTH OCCUPATIONS ARTICLE; OR

(2) A FACILITY THAT PROVIDES HEALTH CARE TO INDIVIDUALS.

(D) "PRIOR AUTHORIZATION" MEANS A UTILIZATION MANAGEMENT TECHNIQUE THAT:

(1) IS USED BY CARRIERS AND MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATIONS;

(2) REQUIRES PRIOR APPROVAL FOR A PROCEDURE, TREATMENT, MEDICATION, OR SERVICE BEFORE AN ENROLLEE IS ELIGIBLE FOR FULL PAYMENT OF THE BENEFIT; AND

(3) IS USED TO DETERMINE WHETHER THE PROCEDURE, TREATMENT, MEDICATION, OR SERVICE IS MEDICALLY NECESSARY.

24-2502.

A AN IN-NETWORK HEALTH CARE PROVIDER MAY NOT CHARGE A FEE TO OBTAIN A PRIOR AUTHORIZATION FROM A CARRIER OR MANAGED CARE ORGANIZATION.

Article - Insurance

15-1012.

(A) (1) IN THIS SECTION THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS INDICATED.

(2) "ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE" HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3.5-801 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.

(3) "PRIOR AUTHORIZATION" HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 1-230 OF THE HEALTH OCCUPATIONS ARTICLE.

(B) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO:

(1) INSURERS AND NONPROFIT HEALTH SERVICE PLANS THAT PROVIDE HOSPITAL, MEDICAL, OR SURGICAL BENEFITS TO INDIVIDUALS OR GROUPS ON AN EXPENSE-INCURRED BASIS UNDER HEALTH INSURANCE POLICIES THAT ARE ISSUED OR DELIVERED IN THE STATE; AND

(2) HEALTH MAINTENANCE ORGANIZATIONS THAT PROVIDE HOSPITAL, MEDICAL, OR SURGICAL BENEFITS TO INDIVIDUALS OR GROUPS UNDER CONTRACTS THAT ARE ISSUED OR DELIVERED IN THE STATE.

(C) AN ENTITY SUBJECT TO THIS SECTION MAY NOT USE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO AUTOMATICALLY DENY A PRIOR AUTHORIZATION.

SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall apply to all policies, contracts, and health benefit plans issued, delivered, or renewed in the State on or after January 1, 2026.

SECTION 3. 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect January 1, 2026.
