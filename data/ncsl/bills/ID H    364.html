LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO

Sixty-eighth Legislature

First Regular Session - 2025

IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

HOUSE BILL NO. 364

BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE

AN ACT

RELATING TO THE DEPARTMENT OF GOVERNMENT EFFICIENCY LEGISLATIVE TASK FORCE; AMENDING CHAPTER 23, TITLE 67, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 67-2362, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH THE DEPARTMENT OF GOVERNMENT EFFICIENCY LEGISLATIVE TASK FORCE; PROVIDING A SUNSET DATE; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY.

Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:

SECTION 1. That Chapter 23, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and designated as Section 67-2362, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:

67-2362. DEPARTMENT OF GOVERNMENT EFFICIENCY LEGISLATIVE TASK FORCE. (1) The state of Idaho recognizes the importance of efficient and responsible governance to best serve the interests of Idaho's citizens. Government inefficiencies, redundancies, and regulatory burdens contribute to unnecessary costs and operational delays and hinder economic growth and effective public service. Proper oversight of state agencies and public expenditures is necessary to ensure fiscal responsibility, prevent waste, and enhance accountability. Therefore, a dedicated legislative task force is necessary to review agency functions, regulatory processes, and budget authority to uphold Idaho's commitment to effective governance.

(2) No later than July 1, 2025, the legislature shall form a department of government efficiency (DOGE) legislative task force that shall consist of nine (9) members as follows:

(a) Three (3) members of the house of representatives appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives;

(b) Three (3) members of the senate appointed by the president pro tempore of the senate; and

(c) Three (3) legislative members from the joint finance-appropriations committee to be appointed by the cochairs of the joint finance-appropriations committee. Of these members, one (1) shall be a member of the house of representatives, one (1) shall be a member of the senate, and the third shall be a member of either the house of representatives or the senate.

(3) Initial DOGE task force members shall be appointed by July 1, 2025, and shall serve until November 30, 2026. Thereafter, DOGE task force members shall serve one (1) year terms. After the initial term, DOGE task force members shall be appointed annually on or before November 30 each year. Task force members may be reappointed. The task force shall retain the membership composition described in subsection (2) of this section for the duration of its existence.

(4) One (1) member appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives shall serve as a cochair of the DOGE task force, and one (1) member appointed by the president pro tempore of the senate shall serve as the other cochair.

(5) The task force shall meet at least twice a year and may be called for special meetings by the cochairs of the task force. Five (5) members shall constitute a quorum. Members of the task force shall be compensated as provided by the citizens' committee on legislative compensation in the same manner as interim legislative meetings, which compensation shall be paid from the legislative account.

(6) The DOGE task force may appoint up to two (2) nonlegislative advisors with technical expertise to assist the committee in efficiently conducting and completing its business. Nonlegislative advisors shall not be reimbursed from legislative funds for per diem, mileage, or other expenses, and shall not have voting privileges regarding the DOGE task force's recommendations or proposed legislation.

(7) The DOGE task force is authorized to study and review governmental efficiency matters pertaining to the state of Idaho in general in order to determine, as applicable, how the legislature may be able to ensure fiscal responsibility, prevent waste, and enhance accountability.

(8) The DOGE task force may divide its work into three (3) subgroups to focus on specific areas of government efficiency:

(a) A regulatory accountability subgroup, which shall focus on evaluating ways to strengthen oversight of state agency rulemaking and making recommendations to ensure Idaho's leadership in regulatory reduction, with a goal of eliminating or shifting nearly all regulations to statute by 2033;

(b) A budget accountability subgroup, which shall focus on conducting base budget reviews, identifying opportunities to eliminate wasteful or redundant government services, programs, and expenditures, and making recommendations to further strengthen budget processes; and

(c) A government operations and oversight subgroup, which shall focus on assessing the structure of state government and recommending agency consolidations or streamlining efforts to optimize efficiency and ensure services are administered by the most appropriate agencies.

(9) The DOGE task force shall from time to time advise the legislature of its findings and recommendations. A copy of any reports, findings, or proposed legislation shall be submitted to the legislative services office electronically. Any subgroup of the DOGE task force shall report any reports, findings, or proposed legislation to the DOGE task force as a whole.

(10) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the task force, acting through the cochairs, may utilize staff and resources within state government. The task force shall make use of technology resources, including artificial intelligence, whenever doing so would increase the DOGE task force's efficiency.

(11) The task force shall operate until November 30, 2029. Thereafter, the DOGE task force shall be dissolved and the provisions of this section shall become null, void, and of no further force and effect.

SECTION 2. The provisions of Section 1 of this act shall be null, void and of no force and effect on and after December 1, 2029.

SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after its passage and approval.
