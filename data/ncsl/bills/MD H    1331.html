Assigned to: Economic Matters

A BILL ENTITLED

AN ACT concerning

Consumer Protection - Artificial Intelligence

FOR the purpose of regulating the manner in which a developer or deployer of artificial intelligence must protect consumers from certain risks; requiring a developer that offers to sell a certain artificial intelligence system to provide certain information and make certain disclosures; requiring a deployer to implement a certain risk management policy and take certain precautions to protect consumers from certain risks; requiring a deployer to complete a certain impact assessment and make certain disclosures; authorizing a deployer to decline certain appeals; providing exceptions to certain requirements for a deployer; establishing a certain rebuttable presumption; making a certain violation an unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practice that is subject to enforcement and penalties under the Maryland Consumer Protection Act; establishing requirements for certain agreements regarding voice and likeness clones; and generally relating to artificial intelligence.

SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND, That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

Article - Commercial Law

13-301.

Unfair, abusive, or deceptive trade practices include any:

(14) Violation of a provision of:

(xlii) Section 12-6C-09.1 of the Health Occupations Article; or

(xliii) Title 14, Subtitle 48 of this article; or

(XLIV) TITLE 14, SUBTITLE 50 OF THIS ARTICLE; OR

SUBTITLE 50. DEVELOPERS AND DEPLOYERS OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS.

14-5001.

(A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS INDICATED.

(B) (1) "ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION" MEANS DIFFERENTIAL TREATMENT AS A RESULT OF THE USE OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE THAT NEGATIVELY IMPACTS A PERSON BASED ON THE PERSON'S ACTUAL OR PERCEIVED AGE, COLOR, DISABILITY, ETHNICITY, GENETIC INFORMATION, PROFICIENCY IN THE ENGLISH LANGUAGE, NATIONAL ORIGIN, RACE, RELIGION, REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH, SEX, VETERAN STATUS, OR OTHER PROTECTED CLASS.

(2) "ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION" DOES NOT INCLUDE:

(I) THE OFFER, LICENSE, OR USE OF A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM BY A DEVELOPER OR DEPLOYER FOR:

1. TESTING TO IDENTIFY, MITIGATE, OR PREVENT DISCRIMINATION OR ENSURE COMPLIANCE WITH A STATE OR FEDERAL LAW; OR

2. INCREASING DIVERSITY WITHIN AN APPLICANT, CUSTOMER, OR PARTICIPANT POOL; OR

(II) AN ACTION THAT BENEFITS AN ESTABLISHMENT THAT:

1. IS SUBJECT TO 42 U.S.C. § 2000 A(E); AND

2. IS NOT IN FACT OPEN TO THE PUBLIC.

(C) "ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE" HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3.5-801 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.

(D) "DATASET CARD" MEANS A FILE THAT:

(1) IS USED TO INFORM USERS ABOUT HOW TO RESPONSIBLY USE THE DATA IN A DATASET; AND

(2) CONTAINS INFORMATION ABOUT POTENTIAL BIASES OF THE DATA.

(E) "DECISION THAT PRODUCES LEGAL OR SIMILARLY SIGNIFICANT EFFECTS CONCERNING THE CONSUMER" HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 14-4701 OF THIS TITLE.

(F) "HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM" MEANS ANY ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM THAT, WHEN DEPLOYED, MAKES, OR IS A SUBSTANTIAL FACTOR IN MAKING, A DECISION THAT PRODUCES LEGAL OR SIMILARLY SIGNIFICANT EFFECTS CONCERNING THE CONSUMER.

(G) "MODEL CARD" MEANS A FILE THAT ACCOMPANIES THE MODEL AND PROVIDES INFORMATION ABOUT DISCOVERABILITY, REPRODUCIBILITY, AND SHARING.

14-5002.

(A) A DEVELOPER SHALL TAKE REASONABLE PRECAUTIONS TO PROTECT CONSUMERS FROM KNOWN OR REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION FROM THE INTENDED USES OF A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM PRODUCED BY THE DEVELOPER.

(B) A DEVELOPER THAT OFFERS TO SELL A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM SHALL:

(1) PROVIDE TO A PURCHASER OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM THE STANDARDIZED DISCLOSURE DOCUMENTATION PERTAINING TO THE USES OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM AS DESCRIBED IN SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION;

(2) PROVIDE TO A DEPLOYER INFORMATION NECESSARY FOR THE DEPLOYER TO COMPLETE AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT UNDER § 14-5004 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND

(3) PUBLISH, ON THE DEVELOPER'S WEBSITE OR IN A PUBLIC USE CASE INVENTORY, STANDARDIZED INFORMATION PERTAINING TO EACH HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM THAT THE DEVELOPER OFFERS FOR PURCHASE.

(C) THE STANDARDIZED DISCLOSURE DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (B)(1) OF THIS SECTION SHALL INCLUDE:

(1) THE PURPOSE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(2) THE INTENDED USES, BENEFITS, AND OUTPUTS OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(3) A DESCRIPTION OF THE KNOWN AND REASONABLY FORESEEABLE:

(I) USES AND MISUSES OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM, AND THE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION ARISING FROM THEM; AND

(II) LIMITATIONS OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(4) A DESCRIPTION OF THE STEPS TAKEN BY THE DEVELOPER PRIOR TO OFFERING A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM FOR PURCHASE TO:

(I) EVALUATE THE PERFORMANCE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM; AND

(II) MITIGATE KNOWN OR REASONABLY FORESEEABLE ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION ARISING FROM THE USE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(5) A SUMMARY OF THE DATA USED TO TRAIN THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(6) A DESCRIPTION OF THE DATA GOVERNANCE MEASURES THAT APPLIED TO THE DATA USED TO TRAIN THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(7) THE MEASURES TAKEN TO EXAMINE:

(I) THE SUITABILITY OF DATA SOURCES; AND

(II) POSSIBLE BIASES AND APPROPRIATE MITIGATION;

(8) BEST PRACTICES FOR THE INTENDED USE, PREVENTION OF MISUSE, AND MONITORING OF A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(9) ANY INFORMATION NECESSARY FOR THE PURCHASER TO COMPLY WITH THE REQUIREMENTS UNDER § 14-5003 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND

(10) ANY INFORMATION REASONABLY NECESSARY TO ALLOW THE PURCHASER TO UNDERSTAND THE OUTPUTS OF AND MONITOR THE PERFORMANCE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM.

(D) THE INFORMATION REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (C)(2) OF THIS SECTION SHALL, TO THE EXTENT FEASIBLE, BE PROVIDED IN THE FORM OF MODEL CARDS, DATASET CARDS, OR PREEXISTING IMPACT ASSESSMENTS.

(E) THE DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED TO BE PUBLISHED UNDER SUBSECTION (C)(3) OF THIS SECTION SHALL:

(1) SUMMARIZE THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS THAT THE DEVELOPER OFFERS FOR SALE;

(2) SUMMARIZE THE MEASURES TAKEN BY THE DEVELOPER TO MANAGE KNOWN AND REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION THAT THE DEVELOPER OFFERS FOR SALE; AND

(3) BE UPDATED:

(I) AS NECESSARY TO ENSURE THAT THE DOCUMENTATION REMAINS ACCURATE; AND

(II) NOT LATER THAN 90 DAYS AFTER ANY CHANGE IS MADE TO A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM THAT WOULD NECESSITATE UPDATING THE RELEVANT DOCUMENTATION UNDER SUBSECTION (C)(3) OF THIS SECTION.

(F) A DEVELOPER THAT LEARNS THROUGH ONGOING INTERNAL TESTING, OR THROUGH A CREDIBLE REPORT, THAT A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM OFFERED FOR SALE BY THE DEVELOPER HAS CAUSED, OR IS LIKELY TO CAUSE, ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION SHALL DISCLOSE THE POTENTIAL FOR ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION TO:

(1) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL IN A FORM AND MANNER DETERMINED BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL; AND

(2) ALL PURCHASERS OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM.

(G) NOTHING IN THIS SECTION MAY BE CONSTRUED TO REQUIRE A DEVELOPER TO DISCLOSE:

(1) A TRADE SECRET;

(2) INFORMATION PROTECTED FROM DISCLOSURE BY STATE OR FEDERAL LAW; OR

(3) INFORMATION THAT WOULD OTHERWISE CREATE A SECURITY RISK TO THE DEVELOPER.

(H) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY REQUIRE A DEVELOPER TO DISCLOSE TO THE ATTORNEY GENERAL THE INFORMATION REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION.

14-5003.

(A) A DEPLOYER SHALL TAKE REASONABLE PRECAUTIONS TO PROTECT CONSUMERS FROM KNOWN OR REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION POSED BY THE INTENDED USES OF A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM DEPLOYED BY THE DEPLOYER.

(B) A DEPLOYER SHALL IMPLEMENT A RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY TO GOVERN THE DEPLOYMENT OF A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM.

(C) A RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION SHALL:

(1) IDENTIFY, DOCUMENT, AND MITIGATE KNOWN AND REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION;

(2) SPECIFY AND INCORPORATE PRINCIPLES, PROCESSES, AND PERSONNEL USED TO COMPLY WITH ITEM (1) OF THIS SUBSECTION;

(3) BE PLANNED TO SPAN, AND BE IMPLEMENTED FOR, THE ENTIRE PERIOD DURING WHICH THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IS TO BE DEPLOYED; AND

(4) BE REGULARLY AND SYSTEMATICALLY REVIEWED AND UPDATED THROUGHOUT THE PERIOD DURING WHICH THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IS DEPLOYED.

(D) A RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION MAY APPLY TO MORE THAN ONE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM.

(E) IN CREATING A RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY UNDER SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, A DEPLOYER SHALL CONSIDER AND REASONABLY ADDRESS:

(1) (I) GUIDANCE SET FORTH IN THE MOST UP-TO-DATE VERSION OF THE "ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK" PUBLISHED BY THE NATIONAL INSTITUTE OF STANDARDS AND TECHNOLOGY;

(II) ISE/IEC 42001 OF THE INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATION FOR STANDARDIZATION;

(III) ANOTHER INTERNATIONALLY RECOGNIZED ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK THAT CONTAINS REQUIREMENTS AT LEAST AS STRINGENT AS THIS SUBTITLE; OR

(IV) ANOTHER RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK DESIGNATED BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL;

(2) THE SIZE AND COMPLEXITY OF THE DEPLOYER;

(3) THE NATURE, USES, AND SCOPE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM; AND

(4) THE SENSITIVITY AND VOLUME OF DATA PROCESSED BY THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM.

14-5004.

(A) EXCEPT AS OTHERWISE PROVIDED IN THIS SUBTITLE, A DEPLOYER SHALL:

(1) COMPLETE AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT OF ANY DEPLOYED HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(2) RETAIN ALL IMPACT ASSESSMENTS AND RECORDS RELATING TO AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT FOR AT LEAST 3 YEARS AFTER THE END OF THE DEPLOYMENT OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(3) NOT LESS THAN ONCE EACH YEAR, ASSESS WHETHER A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM DEPLOYED BY THE DEPLOYER IS CAUSING ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION;

(4) MAKE AVAILABLE, AT THE TIME OF DEPLOYMENT, A STANDARDIZED DISCLOSURE THAT MEETS THE REQUIREMENTS OF § 14-5005 OF THIS SUBTITLE;

(5) PROVIDE A CONSUMER SUBJECT TO A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM DEPLOYED BY THE DEPLOYER AN OPPORTUNITY TO:

(I) CORRECT ANY DATA PROCESSED BY THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IN MAKING A DECISION ABOUT THE CONSUMER; AND

(II) APPEAL AN ADVERSE DECISION MADE USING A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM, INCLUDING HUMAN REVIEW OF THE DECISION; AND

(6) MAKE A CLEAR AND PROMINENTLY DISPLAYED STATEMENT AVAILABLE ON THE DEPLOYER'S WEBSITE THAT:

(I) SUMMARIZES THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS CURRENTLY DEPLOYED BY THE DEPLOYER;

(II) SUMMARIZES THE DEPLOYER'S PRACTICES FOR MANAGING THE KNOWN OR REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION APPLICABLE TO DEPLOYED HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS;

(III) DETAILS THE NATURE, SOURCE, AND EXTENT OF PERSONAL INFORMATION COLLECTED OR USED BY THE DEPLOYER; AND

(IV) IS UPDATED PERIODICALLY.

(B) A DEPLOYER MAY DECLINE TO PROVIDE AN OPPORTUNITY FOR APPEAL IF THE APPEAL WOULD BE AGAINST THE BEST INTERESTS OF THE CONSUMER, INCLUDING SITUATIONS IN WHICH THE DELAY CAUSED BY THE APPEAL COULD POSE A RISK TO THE SAFETY OF THE CONSUMER.

(C) A DEPLOYER NEED NOT PROVIDE A RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY, AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT, OR THE DISCLOSURES REQUIRED UNDER SUBSECTION (A)(6) OF THIS SECTION IF THE DEPLOYER:

(1) EMPLOYS FEWER THAN 50 FULL-TIME EQUIVALENT EMPLOYEES;

(2) TRAINS THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM ON DATA OTHER THAN DATA COLLECTED BY THE DEPLOYER;

(3) DOES NOT TRAIN THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM ON DATA THAT THE DEPLOYER COLLECTS;

(4) USES THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM AS INTENDED BY ITS DEVELOPER, AS DISCLOSED UNDER § 14-5002(C) OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND

(5) MAKES AVAILABLE TO A CONSUMER AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT:

(I) COMPLETED BY AND PROVIDED TO THE DEPLOYER BY THE DEVELOPER OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM; AND

(II) THAT IS SUBSTANTIALLY SIMILAR TO AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT OTHERWISE REQUIRED UNDER THIS SECTION.

14-5005.

A STANDARDIZED DISCLOSURE UNDER § 14-5004(A)(4) OF THIS SUBTITLE SHALL:

(1) NOTIFY A CONSUMER SUBJECT TO THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM THAT A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IS IN USE;

(2) DISCLOSE THE PURPOSE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IN USE, INCLUDING THE NATURE OF A DECISION ABOUT A CONSUMER THAT IS MADE BY THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(3) PROVIDE THE CONTACT INFORMATION OF THE DEPLOYER;

(4) PROVIDE A PLAIN LANGUAGE DESCRIPTION OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(5) PROVIDE INSTRUCTIONS REGARDING HOW TO ACCESS THE STATEMENT REQUIRED UNDER § 14-5004(C)(5) OF THIS SUBTITLE;

(6) DISCLOSE THE DECISION THAT THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IS DEPLOYED TO MAKE OR CONTRIBUTE TO MAKING;

(7) DISCLOSE THE REASON FOR THE DECISION THAT THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IS DEPLOYED TO MAKE OR CONTRIBUTE TO MAKING;

(8) DISCLOSE THE DEGREE TO WHICH THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM IS INVOLVED IN MAKING THE DECISION DISCLOSED UNDER ITEM (6) OF THIS SECTION;

(9) DISCLOSE THE DATA THAT THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM USES IN MAKING OR CONTRIBUTING TO MAKING THE DECISION AND ITS SOURCE;

(10) BE PROVIDED DIRECTLY TO THE CONSUMER, OR IN A MANNER REASONABLY CALCULATED TO BE ACCESSIBLE BY THE CONSUMER IF PROVIDING THE DISCLOSURE DIRECTLY IS NOT POSSIBLE;

(11) BE IN PLAIN LANGUAGE;

(12) BE MADE AVAILABLE IN EACH LANGUAGE THAT THE DEPLOYER REGULARLY USES IN CONTRACTS, DISCLAIMERS, SALE ANNOUNCEMENTS, AND OTHER INFORMATION PROVIDED TO CONSUMERS; AND

(13) BE MADE AVAILABLE IN A FORMAT THAT IS ACCESSIBLE TO CONSUMERS WITH DISABILITIES.

14-5006.

(A) AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT SHALL BE COMPLETED:

(1) AT LEAST ONCE EACH YEAR; AND

(2) WITHIN 90 DAYS AFTER AN INTENTIONAL AND SUBSTANTIAL MODIFICATION OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM.

(B) AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL INCLUDE:

(1) THE PURPOSE, INTENDED USE CASES, AND BENEFITS OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(2) THE CONTEXT IN WHICH THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM WAS DEPLOYED;

(3) AN ANALYSIS OF KNOWN OR REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION POSED BY THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(4) A DESCRIPTION OF STEPS TAKEN TO MITIGATE KNOWN OR REASONABLY FORESEEABLE RISKS OF ALGORITHMIC DISCRIMINATION POSED BY THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(5) A DESCRIPTION OF THE INPUTS AND OUTPUTS OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(6) A DESCRIPTION OF ANY DATA USED BY A DEVELOPER TO CUSTOMIZE THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(7) A DESCRIPTION OF ANY METRICS USED TO EVALUATE THE PERFORMANCE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(8) A DESCRIPTION OF KNOWN LIMITATIONS OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM;

(9) A DESCRIPTION OF TRANSPARENCY MEASURES IMPLEMENTED REGARDING THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM, INCLUDING MEASURES TAKEN TO DISCLOSE THAT THE SYSTEM IS IN USE TO A CONSUMER WHEN THE SYSTEM IS IN USE;

(10) A DESCRIPTION OF THE DEPLOYER'S OVERSIGHT PROCESSES, INCLUDING:

(I) USER SAFEGUARDS PROVIDED; AND

(II) THE DEPLOYER'S PROCESS FOR ADDRESSING ISSUES ARISING FROM THE DEPLOYMENT OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM; AND

(11) FOR ANY IMPACT ASSESSMENT FOLLOWING THE INITIAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT OF A HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM, A DESCRIPTION OF THE EXTENT TO WHICH THE USE OF THE HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM WAS CONSISTENT WITH THE DEVELOPER'S INTENDED USES.

(C) AN IMPACT ASSESSMENT UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION MAY:

(1) ADDRESS MULTIPLE SUBSTANTIALLY SIMILAR HIGH-RISK ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEMS; AND

(2) HAVE BEEN CREATED IN ORDER TO COMPLY WITH ANOTHER REQUIREMENT OTHER THAN THIS SUBTITLE IF THE IMPACT ASSESSMENT SATISFIES THE REQUIREMENTS OF THIS SUBTITLE.

14-5007.

(A) THERE IS A REBUTTABLE PRESUMPTION THAT A DEVELOPER TOOK REASONABLE PRECAUTIONS UNDER § 14-5002(A) OF THIS SUBTITLE IF THE DEVELOPER COMPLIED WITH:

(1) § 14-5002 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND

(2) REGULATIONS ADOPTED BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL.

(B) THERE IS A REBUTTABLE PRESUMPTION THAT A DEPLOYER TOOK REASONABLE PRECAUTIONS UNDER § 14-5003(A) OF THIS SUBTITLE IF THE DEPLOYER COMPLIED WITH:

(1) § 14-5003 OF THIS SUBTITLE; AND

(2) REGULATIONS ADOPTED BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL.

(C) (1) THE ATTORNEY GENERAL MAY, IN A FORM AND MANNER PRESCRIBED BY THE ATTORNEY GENERAL, REQUIRE A DEVELOPER OR DEPLOYER TO PROVIDE DISCLOSURES OTHERWISE REQUIRED UNDER THIS SUBTITLE FOR PURPOSES OF EVALUATING THE DISCLOSURE'S COMPLIANCE WITH THIS SUBTITLE.

(2) A DEVELOPER OR DEPLOYER MAY DECLINE TO PROVIDE THE ATTORNEY GENERAL WITH INFORMATION THAT WOULD REQUIRE THE DISCLOSURE OF TRADE SECRETS OR INFORMATION OTHERWISE PROTECTED BY STATE OR FEDERAL LAW.

(3) INFORMATION PROVIDED TO THE ATTORNEY GENERAL UNDER THIS SUBSECTION IS NOT SUBJECT TO DISCLOSURE UNDER THE MARYLAND PUBLIC INFORMATION ACT.

(4) DISCLOSURE OF INFORMATION UNDER THIS SUBSECTION THAT IS SUBJECT TO ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGE OR WORK-PRODUCT PROTECTION DOES NOT WAIVE THAT PRIVILEGE OR PROTECTION.

14-5008.

(A) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION, A VIOLATION OF THIS SUBTITLE IS:

(1) AN UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICE WITHIN THE MEANING OF TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE; AND

(2) SUBJECT TO THE ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTY PROVISIONS CONTAINED IN TITLE 13 OF THIS ARTICLE, EXCEPT FOR § 13-408 OF THIS ARTICLE.

(B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT PREVENT A CONSUMER FROM PURSUING ANY OTHER REMEDY PROVIDED BY LAW.

SUBTITLE 51. AGREEMENTS CONCERNING VOICE AND LIKENESS CLONES.

14-5101.

(A) IN THIS SUBTITLE THE FOLLOWING WORDS HAVE THE MEANINGS INDICATED.

(B) "ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE" HAS THE MEANING STATED IN § 3.5-801 OF THE STATE FINANCE AND PROCUREMENT ARTICLE.

(C) "DIGITAL COPY" MEANS A NEWLY CREATED, ELECTRONIC REPRESENTATION OF THE IDENTITY OF AN ACTUAL INDIVIDUAL CREATED USING A COMPUTER, AN ALGORITHM, SOFTWARE, A TOOL, ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, OR ANY OTHER TECHNOLOGY THAT IS:

(1) FIXED IN A SOUND RECORDING OR AUDIOVISUAL WORK IN WHICH THE INDIVIDUAL DID NOT ACTUALLY PERFORM OR APPEAR; AND

(2) SO REALISTIC THAT A REASONABLE PERSON WOULD BELIEVE THE DIGITAL COPY IS A PERFORMANCE BY THE INDIVIDUAL BEING PORTRAYED AND NOT ANOTHER INDIVIDUAL.

14-5102.

AN AGREEMENT TO PERFORM PERSONAL OR PROFESSIONAL SERVICES IS UNENFORCEABLE IF:

(1) THE AGREEMENT ALLOWS FOR THE CREATION OR USE OF A DIGITAL COPY OF A PERSON'S VOICE OR IMAGE INSTEAD OF WORK PERFORMED LIVE BY THE PERSON;

(2) THE AGREEMENT DOES NOT INCLUDE:

(I) A DESCRIPTION OF THE INTENDED USES OF THE DIGITAL COPY THAT IS EASILY UNDERSTANDABLE; AND

(II) THE LICENSING TERMS GOVERNING THE USE OF THE DIGITAL COPY; AND

(3) THE PERSON WAS NOT REPRESENTED BY:

(I) A LAWYER WHO NEGOTIATED ON BEHALF OF THE PERSON LICENSING THE RIGHTS TO A DIGITAL COPY; OR

(II) A LABOR UNION REPRESENTING WORKERS WHO PERFORM SIMILAR WORK WITH A COLLECTIVE BARGAINING AGREEMENT THAT EXPRESSLY COVERS THE USE OF DIGITAL COPIES.

Article - State Finance and Procurement

3.5-801.

(a) In this subtitle the following words have the meanings indicated.

(c) "Artificial intelligence" means a machine-based system that:

(1) can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments;

(2) uses machine and human-based inputs to perceive real and virtual environments and abstracts those perceptions into models through analysis in an automated manner; and

(3) uses model inference to formulate options for information or action.

SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect October 1, 2025.
