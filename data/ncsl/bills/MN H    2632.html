A bill for an act relating to crime; providing for the crime of digital picture and recording misrepresentation; proposing coding for new law in Minnesota Statutes, chapter 609.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MINNESOTA:

Section 1. [609.8915] DIGITAL PICTURE AND RECORDING MISREPRESENTATION.

(a) A person is guilty of digital picture and recording misrepresentation if the person intentionally alters a digital picture or recording of another person or owned by another person:

(1) without obtaining the subject person's or owner's consent;

(2) with the intent to cause a recipient of the altered digital picture or recording to reasonably believe that the subject person or owner authorized or transmitted the digital picture or recording by a means including, at a minimum, email, text message, or posting on the Internet; and

(3) with the intent to harm, defraud, or maliciously misrepresent:

(i) the subject person or owner; or

(ii) an event presented, attended, or both, by the subject person or owner, or personal or real property owned by the subject person or owner.

(b) A person who violates paragraph (a) is guilty of a gross misdemeanor.

EFFECTIVE DATE. This section is effective August 1, 2025, and applies to crimes committed on or after that date.
