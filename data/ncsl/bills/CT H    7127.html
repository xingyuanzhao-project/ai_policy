AN ACT CONCERNING STATE AGENCY CONTRACTS FOR THE LICENSING OF SOFTWARE APPLICATIONS.

Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General Assembly convened:

Section 1. Section 4d-30 of the general statutes is repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October 1, 2025):

As used in this section, and sections 4d-31 to 4d-44, inclusive, and section 2 of this act:

(1) "Contract" means a contract for state agency information system or telecommunication system facilities, equipment or services, which is awarded pursuant to this chapter or subsection (e) of section 1-205, subsection (c) of section 1-211, subsection (b) of section 1-212, section 4-5, subsection (a) of section 10a-151b, or subsection (b) of section 19a-110.

(2) "Contractor" means a business entity or individual who is awarded a contract or an amendment to a contract.

(3) "Subcontractor" means a subcontractor of a contractor for work under a contract or an amendment to a contract.

Sec. 2. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2025) Any contract entered into or amended on or after October 1, 2025, for the licensing of software applications that are designed to be run on generally available desktop or server hardware shall not contain any provision limiting a state agency's ability to install or run such software on the hardware of the state agency's choosing.

This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following sections:
