SENATE BILL No. 377

DIGEST OF INTRODUCED BILL

Citations Affected: IC 4-13-2-18.5; IC 36-7.7-1-2.

Synopsis: Funding for regional economic development. Provides certain planning and review requirements that must occur before funding may be allotted and disbursed to a state agency for purposes of regional development. Specifies priorities for regional development funding commitments. Repeals the statute that expires the central Indiana regional development authority.

Effective: Upon passage.

Goode

January 13, 2025, read first time and referred to Committee on Appropriations.

Introduced

First Regular Session of the 124th General Assembly (2025)

SENATE BILL No. 377

A BILL FOR AN ACT to amend the Indiana Code concerning state and local administration.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Indiana:

SECTION 1. IC 4-13-2-18.5 IS ADDED TO THE INDIANA CODE AS A NEW SECTION TO READ AS FOLLOWS [EFFECTIVE UPON PASSAGE]: Sec. 18.5. (a) As used in this section, "regional development authority" includes:

(1) the northwest Indiana regional development authority established by IC 36-7.5-2-1;

(2) a regional development authority established under IC 36-7.6-2-3;

(3) a regional development authority established under IC 36-7.7-3-1;

(4) the northeast Indiana strategic development commission established by IC 36-7-39-3; and

(5) any other similar development authority created by statute.

(b) Notwithstanding any other provision, before money may be allotted and disbursed to a state agency for the purpose of regional economic development, the state agency must:

(1) get advanced approval by the budget committee (before any funding is released) on:

(A) a written plan of implementation (including a timeline);

(B) a sample application for funding; and

(C) the names of any potential selection committee members with individual conflicts of interest statements;

(2) ensure that selection committee meetings are held in public with written meeting minutes and ensure that meeting minutes are shared electronically with members of the budget committee and made available on the state agency's website; and

(3) work with regional development authorities as defined in subsection (a) as the only entities eligible to receive and distribute funding.

(c) Regional economic development funding commitments shall be made by the state agency with the advice of and in consultation with the Indiana Applied Research Institute and based on Endless Frontier priorities that include:

(1) one health life sciences;

(2) emerging biotechnology;

(3) microelectronics;

(4) quantum physics;

(5) artificial intelligence;

(6) cybersecurity; or

(7) defense development.

SECTION 2. IC 36-7.7-1-2 IS REPEALED [EFFECTIVE UPON PASSAGE]. Sec. 2. This article expires July 1, 2025.

SECTION 3. An emergency is declared for this act.
