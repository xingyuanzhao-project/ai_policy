Subject: Consumer protection; weights and measures; dynamic pricing

Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to establish a moratorium on the use of electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing by retailers selling consumer commodities in the State. The moratorium would be effective until January 15, 2027. During the moratorium, the bill would require the Secretary of Agriculture, Food and Markets to report to the General Assembly regarding the use of electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing and how to protect consumers. When the moratorium expires, provisions regarding the use of electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing would go into effect.

An act relating to the use of dynamic pricing by retail establishments

It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:

Sec. 1. 6 V.S.A. § 681 is amended to read:

§ 681. DEFINITIONS BILL AS INTRODUCED

As used in this chapter:

(1) "Secretary" means the Secretary of Agriculture, Food and Markets.

(2) "Consumer commodity" means any food, drug, device, or cosmetic and other article, product, or commodity of any other kind or class, except for drugs sold only by prescription:

(A) that are customarily produced for sale to retail sales agencies or instrumentalities for consumption by individuals, for use by individuals for purposes of personal care, or in the performance of services ordinarily rendered in or around the household; and

(B) that usually are consumed or expended in the course of such consumption or use.

(2) "Dynamic pricing" means the use of artificial intelligence to adjust the prices on the electronic shelf labels of consumer commodities at any given moment.

(3) "Electronic shelf labels"are digital wireless display systems that retailers use to show product prices at the point of sale.

(4) "Point of sale" means the point at which consumer commodities are offered and displayed for retail sale in such a manner that the consumer may examine and select commodities for purchase without the assistance of sales personnel.

(5) "Retailer" means a person who sells a consumer commodity in the State at points of sale.

(6) "Secretary" means the Secretary of Agriculture, Food and Markets.

(3) (7) "Unit price" of a consumer commodity means the retail price of a consumer commodity expressed in terms of the retail price of such commodity per such unit of weight, measure, or count as the Secretary designates, computed to the nearest whole cent or fraction thereof as the Secretary designates.

Sec. 2. 6 V.S.A. § 684 is added to read:

§ 684. USE OF ELECTRONIC SHELF LABELS; DYNAMIC PRICING

(a) A retailer selling a consumer commodity in the State may use electronic shelf labels to disclose to consumers the retail price and unit price of the consumer commodity for sale.

(b) A retailer using electronic shelf labels shall not use dynamic pricing to alter the retail price or unit price of a consumer commodity while the retailer is open to the public.

(c) A retailer shall not alter the retail price or unit price of a consumer commodity until at least 24 hours since the last alteration to the retail price or unit price for the consumer commodity.

Sec. 3. REPORT ON USE OF ELECTRONIC SHELF LABELS DYNAMIC PRICING; MORATORIUM

(a) As used in this section:

(1) "Consumer commodity" means any food, drug, device, or cosmetic and other article, product, or commodity of any other kind or class, except for drugs sold only by prescription:

(A) that are customarily produced for sale to retail sales agencies or instrumentalities for consumption by individuals, for use by individuals for purposes of personal care, or in the performance of services ordinarily rendered in or around the household; and

(B) that usually are consumed or expended in the course of such consumption or use.

(2) "Dynamic pricing" means the use of artificial intelligence to adjust the prices on the electronic shelf labels of consumer commodities at any given moment.

(3) "Electronic shelf labels" are digital wireless display systems that retailers use to show product prices at the point of sale.

(4) "Point of sale" means the point at which consumer commodities are offered and displayed for retail sale in such a manner that the consumer may examine and select commodities for purchase without the assistance of sales personnel.

(5) "Retailer" means a person who sells a consumer commodity in the State at points of sale.

(b) On or before January 15, 2027, a retailer shall not use electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing to display the retail price or unit price of a consumer commodity offered for sale at a point of sale.

(c) On or before January 15, 2026, the Secretary of Agriculture, Food and Markets shall submit to the House Committees on Agriculture, Food Resiliency, and Forestry and on Commerce and Economic Development and the Senate Committees on Agriculture and on Economic Development, Housing and General Affairs a report regarding the use by retailers in the State of electronic shelf labels and dynamic pricing to display or alter the display of the retail price or unit price of a consumer commodity. The report shall include:

(1) a summary of how electronic shelf labels and dynamic pricing operates, including the technology used to operate electronic shelf labels and dynamic pricing;

(2) a summary of national state standards for regulation of the use of electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing, including whether entities such as the National Institute on Standards and Technology have adopted recommended provisions for the regulation of electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing; and

(3) a recommendation on whether and how to use electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing in a manner that protects consumers from unfair pricing strategies or mispricing.

Sec. 4. EFFECTIVE DATES

(a) This section and Sec. 3 (moratorium; report on electronic shelf labels and dynamic pricing) shall take effect on passage.

(b) Secs. 1 and 2 (allowed use of electronic shelf labels or dynamic pricing) shall take effect on January 16, 2027.
