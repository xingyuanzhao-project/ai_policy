Session of 2025

AN ACT

Providing for safety regarding artificial intelligence in companionship applications; and imposing a penalty.

The General Assembly of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania hereby enacts as follows:

Section 1. Short title.

This act shall be known and may be cited as the Artificial Intelligence in Companionship Applications Safety Act.

Section 2. Definitions.

The following words and phrases when used in this act shall have the meanings given to them in this section unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:

"AI companion."

(1) A system that:

(i) uses artificial intelligence and generative artificial intelligence to simulate a human or humanlike relationship with emotional recognition algorithms; and

(ii) interacts with a user by compiling previous information or discussions from user sessions to:

(A) engage with the user's preferences;

(B) personalize interaction based on user preferences;

(C) ask emotion-based questions unprompted in order to illicit feelings; and

(D) maintain conversations to the user feelings or personal matters.

(2) The term does not include AI used for customer service, research, technical assistance or systems for employee productivity in the workplace.

"AI model." A component of an informational system program that uses artificial intelligence for computational techniques to produce outputs from a given set of outputs.

"Artificial intelligence" or "AI." A machine-based system that can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make predictions, recommendations or decisions influencing real or virtual environments, including the ability to:

(1) perceive real and virtual environments;

(2) abstract perceptions made under paragraph (1) into AI models through analysis in an automated manner; and

(3) use AI model inference to formulate options for information or action based on outcomes under paragraphs (1) and (2).

"Emotional recognition algorithms." Artificial intelligence that detects and reacts to an individual user's emotions from collected data like text, voice audio or video.

"Generative artificial intelligence." The class of AI models that emulate the structure and characteristics of input data in order to generate derived synthetic content.

"Operator." Any individual, association, business, member or subsidiary who operates for or provides an AI companion to a user.

"Self-harm." Intentional self-injury.

"Suicidal ideation." A feeling, thought or consideration with the preoccupation of the idea of death or suicide.

"Synthetic content." Information, such as images, videos, audio clips or text, that have been significantly modified or generated by algorithms, including by artificial intelligence.

"User." An individual who:

(1) uses an AI companion for personal use within this Commonwealth; and

(2) is not an operator.

Section 3. Artificial intelligence companion.

(a) Certain protocols required.--It shall be unlawful for an operator to provide an AI companion to a user unless the AI companion contains protocols that:

(1) identify suicidal ideation or expressions of self-harm;

(2) decline to assist a user with a suicide attempt, methods or improvement of methods; and

(3) refer the user to a crisis center if suicidal ideation or expressions of self-harm are recognized.

(b) Referral to crisis center.--The referral required under subsection (a)(3) shall include:

(1) crisis service contact information, including the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline, or a subsequent iteration;

(2) the closest behavioral health crisis centers to the user; or

(3) other appropriate crisis services.

(c) Prohibition.--An AI companion may not claim, imply or advertise that the AI companion is a licensed emotional support professional or mental health professional or replaces services rendered by a licensed mental health professional.

Section 4. Artificial intelligence companion requirements.

An operator shall:

(1) Publish details on the protocol on the operator's Internet website.

(2) At the beginning of a session with an AI companion and once every three hours during the session, provide a notification to the user stating, either verbally or in writing, that the user is communicating with an AI companion and not a human.

Section 5. Violations and penalty.

A user may file a complaint with the Attorney General alleging that an operator has violated this act, to which the following shall apply:

(1) The Attorney General, if provided satisfactory evidence that an operator has, or intends to, violate this act, can bring an action in the name and on behalf of the people of this Commonwealth against the operator.

(2) An operator in violation of this act shall be subject to:

(i) a civil penalty of no more than $15,000 per day per violation; and

(ii) additional remedies that the court deems appropriate.

(3) The Attorney General may initiate, in Commonwealth Court or the court of common pleas of the county in which the individual or entity resides, an action in equity for an injunction to restrain a violation of this act, to which the following shall apply:

(i) The injunction may be issued by a court without requiring proof that an individual has been injured or experienced harm.

(ii) The respondent shall comply with the injunction within five days.

Section 6. Effective date.

This act shall take effect in 60 days.
