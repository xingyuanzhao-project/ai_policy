HB2655

Introduced, by Rep. Abdelnasser Rashid

SYNOPSIS AS INTRODUCED:

110 ILCS 205/9.45 new

Amends the Board of Higher Education Act. Requires the Board of Higher Education to prepare and submit to the General Assembly a report on the state of artificial intelligence education and development in public and private institutions of higher education. Sets forth what information the report must contain. Repeals the provisions 2 years after the effective date of the amendatory Act.

A BILL FOR

AN ACT concerning education.

Be it enacted by the People of the State of Illinois, represented in the General Assembly:

Section 5. The Board of Higher Education Act is amended by adding Section 9.45 as follows:

(110 ILCS 205/9.45 new)

Sec. 9.45. Artificial intelligence education report.

(a) Within 18 months after the effective date of this amendatory Act of the 104th General Assembly, the Board shall prepare and submit to the General Assembly a report on the state of artificial intelligence education and development in public and private institutions of higher education. The report shall contain, at a minimum, all of the following information:

(1) All existing academic programs and courses focused on educating students about the use, development, and ethical and social implications of artificial intelligence systems.

(2) All future plans for implementing such programs and courses.

(3) All current and future plans for research projects studying the use, development, and implications of artificial intelligence.

(4) All current and future plans for the development of artificial intelligence systems in public and private institutions of higher education.

(5) Recommendations to the General Assembly to further develop programs and course offerings studying artificial intelligence.

(6) Recommendations on the best way to implement a State-funded grant program to promote the academic study and commercial development of artificial intelligence in public and private institutions of higher education and to fulfill all of the following objectives:

(A) To develop labs to provide students hands-on, artificial intelligence learning experiences.

(B) To develop virtual learning platforms that facilitate remote and individualized artificial intelligence education opportunities.

(C) To develop programming and pedagogical tools with respect to artificial intelligence education and instruction for the benefit of elementary and secondary school teachers and community education.

(b) This Section is repealed 2 years after the effective date of this amendatory Act of the 104th General Assembly.
