CS FOR HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 3

IN THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA

THIRTY-FOURTH LEGISLATURE - FIRST SESSION

A RESOLUTION

Establishing the Joint Legislative Task Force on Artificial Intelligence.

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF ALASKA:

WHEREAS artificial intelligence is a rapidly advancing technology with significant implications for government operations, economic development, public services, and data security; and

WHEREAS artificial intelligence is being integrated into various sectors, including public safety, transportation, healthcare, and education, and artificial intelligence has the potential to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of state government operations; and

WHEREAS the development and use of artificial intelligence and the establishment of data centers in the state could stimulate economic growth, create job opportunities, and position the state as a leader in technological innovation; and

WHEREAS the use of artificial intelligence and generated images raises important ethical considerations, including data privacy, security, algorithmic transparency, and the potential for bias and abuse, all of which necessitates careful legislative oversight; and

WHEREAS understanding the role of artificial intelligence in government and society is essential to ensuring its use benefits residents of the state while mitigating potential risks to civil liberties and public trust; and

WHEREAS artificial intelligence includes a diverse and growing set of tools and technology, including expert systems, machine learning, generative agents, new hardware platforms and software systems;

BE IT RESOLVED that the Alaska State Legislature establishes the Joint Legislative Task Force on Artificial Intelligence to examine the development, implementation, and regulation of artificial intelligence in the state; and be it

FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall

(1) create a set of common terms and definitions as a foundation for the work of the task force and future legislation;

(2) evaluate current and potential applications and costs of artificial intelligence in state government operations, including data management, public service delivery, decision-making processes, efficiency, productivity improvements, and staffing vacancies;

(3) assess the economic opportunities associated with artificial intelligence development, including entrepreneurship and new venture creation and the establishment and operation of data centers and technology hubs in the state;

(4) investigate ethical, legal, and privacy concerns related to artificial intelligence use, including the use of generated images, in public and private sectors;

(5) explore workforce implications, including the need for training programs and education initiatives to prepare residents of the state for artificial intelligence-related employment opportunities;

(6) recommend policies and legislation to ensure the responsible and secure use of artificial intelligence inside and outside state government; and

(7) develop a long-term strategic plan and recommended budgets for fostering artificial intelligence innovation while safeguarding public interests in the state; and be it

FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force consists of seven members, appointed as follows:

(1) three members from the senate, appointed by the president of the senate, including one member designated as vice-chair of the task force; and

(2) four members from the house of representatives, appointed by the speaker of the house of representatives, including one member designated as chair of the task force; and be it

FURTHER RESOLVED that

(1) the chair of the task force shall preside over the task force and coordinate its activities, ensuring an equal representation of both legislative bodies in the decision- making process;

(2) the members of the task force shall objectively evaluate and support the responsible use and development of artificial intelligence statewide, without focusing solely on specific regions, vendors, or industries;

(3) staff of the president of the senate, the speaker of the house, and the Legislative Affairs Agency may provide administrative and other support to the task force;

(4) the task force shall meet at the call of the chair and, to the extent practicable, conduct meetings virtually or through other electronic means;

(5) a majority of the task force constitutes a quorum; and be it

FURTHER RESOLVED that, by January 31, 2026, the task force shall submit a report of its findings and recommendations to the senate secretary and the chief clerk of the house of representatives and notify the legislature that the report is available; the task force may make any interim reports the task force considers advisable; and be it

FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force expires on February 2, 2026.
