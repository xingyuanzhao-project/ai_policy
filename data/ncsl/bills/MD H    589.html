HOUSE BILL 589

By: Delegates Grammer and Arikan

Introduced and read first time: January 23, 2025

Assigned to: Judiciary

A BILL ENTITLED

AN ACT concerning

Artificial Intelligence - Causing Injury or Death - Civil and Criminal Liability

FOR the purpose of establishing that a person who intentionally, knowingly, or negligently designs or creates artificial intelligence software able to cause physical injury or death is strictly liable for damages and subject to a civil penalty if the software is used to cause personal injury or death; prohibiting a person from intentionally, knowingly, or negligently designing or creating artificial intelligence software able to cause injury or death; and generally relating to prohibitions relating to artificial intelligence.

BY adding to

Article - Courts and Judicial Proceedings

Section 3-2601 to be under the new subtitle "Subtitle 26. Personal Injury or Death Caused by Implementation of Artificial Intelligence Software"

BY adding to

Section 1-403

Annotated Code of Maryland

SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND, That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

Article - Courts and Judicial Proceedings

SUBTITLE 26. PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH CAUSED BY IMPLEMENTATION OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SOFTWARE.

3-2601.

A PERSON WHO INTENTIONALLY, KNOWINGLY, OR NEGLIGENTLY DESIGNS OR CREATES ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SOFTWARE WITH THE CAPABILITY OF CAUSING PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH IS:

(1) STRICTLY LIABLE FOR DAMAGES RESULTING FROM PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH CAUSED BY THE USE OF THE SOFTWARE; AND

(2) SUBJECT TO A CIVIL PENALTY NOT EXCEEDING THE APPLICABLE LIMITATION ON NONECONOMIC DAMAGES UNDER § 3-2A-09 OF THIS TITLE IF THE USE OF THE SOFTWARE CAUSES PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH.

Article - Criminal Law

1-403.

(A) (1) A PERSON MAY NOT INTENTIONALLY DESIGN OR CREATE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SOFTWARE WITH THE CAPABILITY OF CAUSING PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH.

(2) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SUBSECTION IS GUILTY OF A FELONY AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 20 YEARS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $100,000 OR BOTH.

(B) (1) A PERSON MAY NOT KNOWINGLY DESIGN OR CREATE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SOFTWARE WITH THE CAPABILITY OF CAUSING PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH.

(2) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SUBSECTION IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 10 YEARS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $50,000 OR BOTH.

(C) (1) A PERSON MAY NOT NEGLIGENTLY DESIGN OR CREATE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SOFTWARE WITH THE CAPABILITY OF CAUSING PERSONAL INJURY OR DEATH.

(2) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SUBSECTION IS GUILTY OF A MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT EXCEEDING 5 YEARS OR A FINE NOT EXCEEDING $25,000 OR BOTH.

SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect October 1, 2025.
