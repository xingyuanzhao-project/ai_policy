H.R. No. 795

RESOLUTION

WHEREAS, A distinguished delegation from Rice University is gathering in Austin on April 14, 2025, to celebrate Rice University Day at the State Capitol; and

WHEREAS, Founded in 1891, Rice University is ranked among the nation's foremost institutions and holds the highest research activity designation, R1, from the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education; during the 2024-2025 academic year, it is marking the 50th anniversaries of four of its schools; and

WHEREAS, The Jones Graduate School of Business is named after its founder, Jesse H. Jones; today, it offers eight prestigious graduate programs and undergraduate options and serves over 1,700 students; under the leadership of Dean Peter Rodriguez, the school has launched four new programs that address the complexities of the modern business landscape; and

WHEREAS, The namesake of the Shepherd School of Music, Benjamin A. Shepherd, was the grandfather of philanthropist Sallie Shepherd Perkins; her endowment laid the foundation for what would become one of the country's premier music schools; each year, it offers more than 400 free concerts and recitals; since 2021, it has been guided by Dean Matthew Loden; and

WHEREAS, While engineering has long been a key component of Rice's legacy, the engineering school itself was established in 1975; it was named the George R. Brown School of Engineering and Computing in 2024 to reflect its heightened focus on computing, artificial intelligence, and machine learning; its faculty, academic programs, and research impact have increased through the dedicated efforts of Dean Luay Nakhleh; and

WHEREAS, The Wiess School of Natural Sciences was created through the generosity of Olga and Harry Wiess; internationally renowned, the faculty has included Nobel Prize winners Robert Curl and Richard Smalley; in continuing a proud tradition of dynamic leadership, Dean Thomas Killian is aided by Associate Dean for Research Douglas Natelson; the school often collaborates with the Texas Medical Center as it prepares students for important roles in such sectors as bioscience, health policy, environmental analysis, and energy; and

WHEREAS, Throughout its history, Rice University has advanced knowledge and innovation in a wide range of disciplines, and in so doing, it has contributed immeasurably to the prosperity and vibrancy of the Lone Star State; now, therefore, be it

RESOLVED, That the House of Representatives of the 89th Texas Legislature hereby recognize April 14, 2025, as Rice University Day at the State Capitol and extend a warm welcome to the visiting delegation; and, be it further

RESOLVED, That an official copy of this resolution be prepared for the university as an expression of high regard by the Texas House of Representatives.
