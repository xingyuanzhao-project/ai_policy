A BILL FOR AN ACT

CONCERNING THE USE OF ARTIFICIAL-INTELLIGENCE-ENABLED DETECTION COMPONENTS TO MITIGATE WILDFIRES.

Bill Summary

The bill requires the division of fire prevention and control (division) in the department of public safety (department) to establish public-private agreements with one or more private partners, by which agreements the state may allocate responsibility or risk to one or more private partners to develop and operate wildfire detection components. The bill also creates the front line innovation and response efficiency cash fund (FIRE fund) in the state treasury. The money in the FIRE fund is annually appropriated to the department to be expended by the division for the purposes of the bill.

In current law, money in the unused state-owned real property fund is continuously appropriated to the department of personnel for several purposes, including paying for public-private agreements and associated costs. Of the money that is appropriated for this purpose, the bill requires the general assembly to transfer the following amounts to the FIRE fund:

! For the 2025-26 state fiscal year, up to $1,000,000;

! For the 2026-27 state fiscal year, $2,000,000; and

! For the 2027-28 state fiscal year, $3,000,000.

The department is required to include information concerning the division's activities under the bill in the department's annual report to the legislative subject matter committees.

Be it enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Colorado:

SECTION 1. Legislative declaration. (1) The general assembly finds that:

(a) Colorado has experienced a sharp rise in wildfire activity in recent years, including the 20 largest fires and the 5 most destructive fires in the state's history;

(b) The growing size, duration, intensity, costs, and impacts of wildfires create a dangerous environment that endangers firefighters and threatens entire communities;

(c) In response, the division of fire prevention and control within the department of public safety has collaborated with local fire departments, counties, and federal partners to develop more effective and efficient wildfire preparedness and response strategies;

(d) These efforts include adopting cutting-edge technologies such as wildfire detection cameras that are designed to quickly identify potential threats, ensure faster response times, and enhance firefighter safety;

(e) So far, the existing network of more than 40 cameras deployed across Colorado has proven highly valuable;

(f) These cameras provide on-demand visual intelligence that allows officials to swiftly assess emerging incidents, coordinate with jurisdictional agencies, and efficiently direct critical air and ground resources to the right locations;

(g) The integration of wildfire cameras with artificial intelligence, or "AI", can play a crucial role in safely and efficiently increasing the use of prescribed fires;

(h) AI-integrated cameras provide multiple vantage points to detect and pinpoint fire activity, monitor fires, and support fire managers in executing prescribed fire projects; and

(i) AI technology is an invaluable asset in improving forest health and reducing wildfire risks, benefitting communities as well as fire management agencies.

(2) The general assembly therefore declares that the safety of the people and property within the state is served by the use of AI-integrated cameras by the division of fire prevention and control within the department of public safety.

SECTION 2. In Colorado Revised Statutes, 24-33.5-1228, add (2.5)(f) as follows:

24-33.5-1228. Colorado firefighting air corps - creation - powers - aircraft acquisitions required - center of excellence - unmanned aircraft systems study and pilot program - Colorado firefighting air corps fund - creation - report - deployment of detection components - rules - definitions. (2.5) (f) (I) AS USED IN THIS SUBSECTION (2.5)(f), UNLESS THE CONTEXT OTHERWISE REQUIRES:

(A) "CAMERA STATION" MEANS ONE OR MORE CAMERAS AND ASSOCIATED HARDWARE THAT ARE CAPABLE OF GENERATING A THREE-HUNDRED-SIXTY-DEGREE PANORAMIC VISUAL FEED AND UTILIZING PAN, TILT, AND TWENTY TIMES OR GREATER ZOOM OPERATIONS.

(B) "DETECTION COMPONENT" MEANS AN ARTIFICIAL-INTELLIGENCE-ENABLED WILDFIRE DETECTION AND INTELLIGENCE SOLUTION THAT INCLUDES A COHESIVE, INTERCONNECTED COLLECTION OF CAMERA STATIONS, ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE DETECTION CAPABILITIES, SOFTWARE, AND COMPREHENSIVE SERVICES.

(C) "END USER" MEANS AN INDIVIDUAL WHO IS AUTHORIZED BY A VENDOR AGREEMENT TO HAVE ACCESS TO THE LIVE FEED OF A DETECTION COMPONENT.

(D) "FUND" MEANS THE FIRE TECHNOLOGY CASH FUND CREATED IN SUBSECTION (2.5)(f)(IV) OF THIS SECTION.

(E) "PRESCRIBED FIRE" HAS THE MEANING SET FORTH IN SECTION 29-22.5-102 (7).

(F) "VENDOR AGREEMENT" MEANS AN AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE DIVISION AND ONE OR MORE QUALIFIED VENDORS, WHICH AGREEMENT REQUIRES THE VENDOR TO COMMUNICATE TO EACH END USER THE REQUIREMENTS OF SECTION 18-7-801 CONCERNING CRIMINAL INVASION OF PRIVACY, CONTAINS A PROVISION FOR PROVIDING PUBLIC NOTICE BY THE VENDOR, ALLOWS THE STATE TO ALLOCATE RESPONSIBILITY OR RISK TO ONE OR MORE VENDORS TO DEVELOP AND OPERATE A DETECTION COMPONENT, AND DEFINES THE SCOPE OF SUCH RESPONSIBILITY OR RISK.

(II) (A) AS NEEDED, THE DIVISION MAY ISSUE A REQUEST FOR QUALIFICATIONS FROM PRIVATE ENTITIES FOR THE PURPOSES OF THIS SECTION. THE REQUEST FOR QUALIFICATIONS MUST SEEK PROPOSALS FOR A DETECTION COMPONENT THAT CAN BE PROCURED AS A COMPREHENSIVE SERVICE PROVIDED BY A VENDOR TO ENSURE THAT THE DEPLOYMENT, OPERATION, AND MAINTENANCE OF THE DETECTION COMPONENTS ARE PERFORMED BY THE VENDOR.

(B) THE DIVISION SHALL DEVELOP A LIST OF THE MOST QUALIFIED VENDORS THAT SUBMIT PROPOSALS PURSUANT TO SUBSECTION (2.5)(f)(II)(A) OF THIS SECTION.

(C) THE DIVISION MAY ESTABLISH VENDOR AGREEMENTS WITH ONE OR MORE OF THE VENDORS THAT SUBMIT PROPOSALS PURSUANT TO SUBSECTION (2.5)(f)(II)(A) OF THIS SECTION.

(III) A DETECTION COMPONENT DEPLOYED PURSUANT TO THIS SECTION MUST:

(A) BE PLACED IN A LOCATION THAT ENABLES TRIANGULATION OF CAMERA STATIONS AND OTHER TECHNOLOGY TO PROVIDE PRECISE LOCATIONS OF FIRES DETECTED BY THE STATEWIDE COVERAGE NETWORK OF DETECTION COMPONENTS;

(B) PROVIDE A LIVE FEED OF INFORMATION THAT CAN DISCOVER, LOCATE, AND CONFIRM IGNITION OF A FIRE, WHICH FEED REFRESHES AT LEAST ONCE PER MINUTE AND IS MONITORED BY AN ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE SYSTEM CAPABLE OF IDENTIFYING WILDFIRE IGNITIONS AT A DISTANCE OF AT LEAST TEN MILES;

(C) MONITOR FIRE BEHAVIOR;

(D) PROVIDE ENHANCED SITUATIONAL AWARENESS FOR EVACUATION AND FIRE RESPONSE ACTIVITIES;

(E) ENSURE THAT CONTAINED AND PRESCRIBED FIRES ARE MONITORED APPROPRIATELY;

(F) INCLUDE A SOFTWARE INTERFACE FOR FIRE AGENCIES AND OTHER END USERS THAT ALLOWS FOR REAL-TIME FIRE DETECTION ALERTS, THREE-HUNDRED-SIXTY-DEGREE CAMERA STATION VIEWS, AND ZOOM CAPABILITY AS WELL AS OTHER NECESSARY MONITORING FUNCTIONALITY; AND

(G) USE PIXELATION OR ANOTHER BLURRING TECHNOLOGY TO PROTECT THE PRIVACY OF PRIVATELY OWNED, HUMAN-MADE STRUCTURES.

(IV) (A) THE FIRE TECHNOLOGY CASH FUND IS CREATED IN THE STATE TREASURY. THE FUND CONSISTS OF ANY MONEY THAT THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY MAY APPROPRIATE OR TRANSFER TO THE FUND FOR THE PURPOSES OF THIS SECTION. THE MONEY IN THE FUND IS ANNUALLY APPROPRIATED TO THE DEPARTMENT TO BE EXPENDED BY THE DIVISION FOR UTILIZATION OF FIRE DETECTION RESPONSE AND MANAGEMENT TECHNOLOGIES, DEPLOYMENT OF DETECTION COMPONENTS THROUGH VENDOR AGREEMENTS, AND UTILIZATION OF TECHNOLOGICAL TOOLS THAT ENABLE ADVANCEMENT IN FIRE DETECTION AND MITIGATION PRACTICES.

(B) THE STATE TREASURER SHALL CREDIT ALL INTEREST AND INCOME DERIVED FROM THE DEPOSIT AND INVESTMENT OF MONEY IN THE FUND TO THE FUND. ANY MONEY REMAINING IN THE FUND AT THE END OF A STATE FISCAL YEAR REMAINS IN THE FUND.

(C) THE DEPARTMENT MAY SEEK, ACCEPT, AND EXPEND GIFTS, GRANTS, AND DONATIONS TO PAY THE COSTS OF IMPLEMENTING THIS SECTION. ANY GIFTS, GRANTS, AND DONATIONS RECEIVED BY THE DEPARTMENT SHALL BE CREDITED TO THE FUND. NOTWITHSTANDING ANY PROVISION OF THIS SUBSECTION (2.5)(f) TO THE CONTRARY, THE DIVISION SHALL NOT ENTER INTO A VENDOR AGREEMENT THAT COMMITS THE DIVISION TO CONTINUE FUNDING FOR A TECHNOLOGY PURCHASED THROUGH A SINGLE GIFT, GRANT, OR DONATION.

(V) IN ITS ANNUAL REPORT TO THE LEGISLATIVE COMMITTEES OF REFERENCE PURSUANT TO SECTION 2-7-203, THE DEPARTMENT SHALL INCLUDE INFORMATION CONCERNING THE ACTIVITIES OF THE DIVISION PURSUANT TO THIS SECTION DURING THE PRECEDING YEAR.

SECTION 3. Act subject to petition - effective date. This act takes effect at 12:01 a.m. on the day following the expiration of the ninety-day period after final adjournment of the general assembly; except that, if a referendum petition is filed pursuant to section 1 (3) of article V of the state constitution against this act or an item, section, or part of this act within such period, then the act, item, section, or part will not take effect unless approved by the people at the general election to be held in November 2026 and, in such case, will take effect on the date of the official declaration of the vote thereon by the governor.
