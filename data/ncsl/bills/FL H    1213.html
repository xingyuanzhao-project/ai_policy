CS/HB 1213

2025

A bill to be entitled

An act relating to a K-12 school route optimization pilot program; amending s. 1006.23, F.S.; creating a K-12 school route optimization pilot program in specified county school districts; providing program requirements; providing reporting requirements; requiring the Department of Education to assist school districts with the implementation of the pilot program; providing for the expiration of the program; providing an effective date.

Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Florida:

Section 1. Subsection (7) is added to section 1006.23, Florida Statutes, to read:

1006.23 Hazardous walking conditions.—

(7) ROUTE OPTIMIZATION PILOT PROGRAM.—There is created a K-12 school route optimization pilot program in Duval, Highlands, Hillsborough, Okeechobee, and Pasco County school districts to model and evaluate the efficiencies and financial impacts of different routing scenarios.

(a) Each school district must use its existing transportation management system in conjunction with a student transportation intelligence platform with artificial intelligence, if available, to evaluate diverse routing scenarios and optimize student transportation efficiency while maintaining student safety. The routing scenario evaluations must include:

1. Expanding school transportation to students in grades 7-12, if such students are subject to current hazardous walking conditions as provided in s. 1006.23 while en route to or from school.

2. Expanding school transportation to students in kindergarten through grade 12 whose homes are more than 1 mile from the nearest appropriate school.

3. Reducing hazardous walking conditions that require students to walk:

a. Along a roadway within 4 feet of lanes of traffic;

b. Along a roadway that is 30 miles per hour or greater;

c. In the dark; or

d. Along a path on which there is no sidewalk adjacent to the road.

4. Dynamically changing routes if a student's route is within 3 miles of the residence of a person registered as a sexual predator under s. 775.21 or as a sexual offender under s. 943.0435.

(b) Each school district shall report the costs to implement each of the requirements and the cost savings, if any, associated with the use of artificial intelligence, including reduction in fuel use, accidents, injuries, the number of buses required to provide transportation, the number or length of routes, and any changes to existing routes based on the use of artificial intelligence or routing scenarios.

(c) The department shall assist school districts with the implementation of the pilot program and submit a summary report of the evaluations submitted by each school district to the Legislature by September 1, 2026.

(d) This subsection is repealed July 1, 2027.

Section 2. This act shall take effect July 1, 2025.
