HB 659

2026

A bill to be entitled

An act relating to interactions with artificial intelligence; creating s. 501.172, F.S.; providing definitions; requiring operators of companion chatbot platforms to display specified information on any application, browser, or format used to access companion chatbots on the platform and to prevent such chatbots from interacting with users unless specified protocols are maintained; requiring such operators to verify the age of users and to take specified actions for users who are minors; requiring such operators to file specified reports with the Department of Legal Affairs; providing that a violation constitutes an unfair or deceptive act or practice; providing for civil actions; providing construction; providing an effective date.

Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Florida:

Section 1. Section 501.172, Florida Statutes, is created to read:

501.172 Companion chatbots.--

(1) As used in this section, the term:

(a) "AI-generated" means any image, video, or audio that is generated or modified by artificial intelligence which may portray realistic human likenesses or depictions.

(b) "Companion chatbot" means an artificial intelligence system with a natural language interface that provides adaptive, human-like responses to user inputs and is capable of meeting a user's social needs, including by exhibiting anthropomorphic features and being able to sustain a relationship across multiple interactions. The term does not include:

1. A bot used only for customer service, business operational purposes, or productivity and analysis related to source information, internal research, or technical assistance;

2. A bot that is a feature of a video game and is limited to replies related to the video game and that cannot discuss topics related to mental health, self-harm, sexually explicit conduct, or maintain a dialogue on other topics unrelated to the video game; or

3. A stand-alone consumer electronic device that functions as a speaker and voice command interface, acts as a voice-activated virtual assistant, and does not sustain a relationship across multiple interactions or generate outputs that are likely to elicit emotional responses in the user.

(c) "Operator" means a person who makes a companion chatbot platform available to a user in this state.

(d) "Sexually explicit conduct" means actual or simulated sexual intercourse, bestiality, masturbation, sadistic or masochistic abuse, or lascivious exhibition of the genitals or pubic area.

(2) An operator shall clearly and conspicuously display the following notice on any application, browser, or other format used to access companion chatbots on the operator's platform: "Companion chatbots are AI-generated and not human. Companion chatbots may not be suitable for some minors."

(3)(a) An operator shall prevent a companion chatbot on the platform from interacting with users unless the operator maintains a protocol to prevent the production of suicidal ideation, suicide, or self-harm content.

(b) At a minimum, the protocol must include a notification that refers the user to the 988 suicide and crisis lifeline call center if the user expresses suicidal ideation, or intent to commit suicide or to self-harm.

(c) The protocol must include mechanisms to:

1. Detect, remove, and respond to expressions of suicidal ideation, or intent to commit suicide or to self-harm by users.

2. Prohibit a companion chatbot from discussing suicidal ideation, suicide, or self-harm with users.

3. Measure suicidal ideation by using evidence-based methods.

(d) The operator shall publish the protocol on the platform.

(4)(a) An operator must offer anonymous age verification and standard age verification pursuant to s. 501.1737 for user access to a companion chatbot on the platform.

(b) For a user the operator knows is a minor, the operator shall do all of the following:

1. Disclose to the user that he or she is interacting with artificial intelligence.

2. Provide a clear and conspicuous default notification at least every 3 hours for continuing companion chatbot interactions reminding the user to take a break from the interaction and that the companion chatbot is AI-generated and not human.

3. Implement reasonable measures to prevent a companion chatbot from producing visual material of sexually explicit conduct or directly stating that the user should engage in sexually explicit conduct.

(5)(a) Beginning July 1, 2027, each operator shall submit to the Department of Legal Affairs an annual report that includes all of the following:

1. The number of times in the preceding calendar year the operator has issued a 988 suicide and crisis lifeline call center referral notification.

2. The protocol for detecting, removing, and responding to expressions of suicidal ideation, or intent to committee suicide or to self-harm by users.

3. The protocol for prohibiting a companion chatbot from discussing suicidal ideation, suicide, or self-harm with users.

(b) The report may not include any identifiers or personal information about users.

(6) A violation of this section constitutes an unfair or deceptive act or practice as specified in s. 501.204 and shall be enforced by civil action by the Attorney General. The Department of Legal Affairs must notify the operator of any suspected violation and allow the operator 30 calendar days to cure the alleged violation before initiating enforcement action. This section does not create a private right of action.

(7) This section does not relieve an operator from any duties, remedies, or obligations imposed by any other law.

Section 2. This act shall take effect July 1, 2026.
